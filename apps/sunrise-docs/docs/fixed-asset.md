The Fixed Asset section allows you to add, edit, and manage the assets within your workspace. This section is essential for keeping detailed records of all tangible and intangible assets owned by your organization. It ensures that you have complete visibility over your assets, from acquisition to disposal.

## üÜï Adding a New Asset

Once you have set up your workspace, go to the Fixed Asset section and click the button in the top right corner to add a new asset. This process allows you to input detailed information, ensuring comprehensive tracking and management of your assets. Follow these steps to efficiently add a new fixed asset:

### Fill in the Basic Information

- **Name**: Provide a clear and concise name for the asset.
- **Code**: Assign a unique code to the asset for easy identification.
- **Serial Number**: Enter the serial number for precise tracking.
- **Tags**: Add relevant tags to categorize the asset.
- **Type**: Specify whether the asset is a tangible or intangible asset.

### Include Visual and Supporting Files

- **Images**: Upload images of the asset for visual reference and identification.
- **Thumbnail**: Select a thumbnail image to represent the asset in lists and overviews.
- **Attachments**: Attach any relevant documents, such as purchase invoices, user manuals, or warranty certificates.

### Assign Defaults

- **Default Location**:
  - **Create Location First**: Ensure the location is already created in the system before assigning it. This helps in organizing assets by their physical or intended location.
- **Default Assignee**:
  - **Create Assignee First**: Assign an individual or department responsible for the asset. Make sure the assignee exists in the system.
- **Lifecycle**:
  - **Create Lifecycle First**: Define the asset's lifecycle stage, such as "New," "In Use," or "Retired." Ensure the lifecycle stages are predefined in the system.

### Input Financial and Date Details

- **Purchase Cost**: Input the purchase cost of the asset (e.g., "$1,500").
- **Purchase Date**: Record the date of purchase for historical tracking (e.g., "March 10, 2024").
- **Warranty Expire Date**: Enter the warranty expiration date to manage maintenance and service contracts effectively (e.g., "March 10, 2026").

### Categorize and Source the Asset

- **Category**:
  - **Create Category First**: Classify the asset into a broader category. Ensure categories are defined in the system before assigning.
- **Supplier**:
  - **Create Supplier First**: Indicate the supplier or vendor from whom the asset was purchased. Ensure suppliers are set up in the system.

### Provide Additional Descriptions

- **Descriptions**: Offer detailed descriptions if necessary, including specific configurations, usage instructions, or additional features.

## Important Considerations

- **Predefine Necessary Data**: Before adding a fixed asset, ensure that locations, assignees, lifecycle stages, categories, and suppliers are created in the system. This preparation allows for seamless assignment and tracking.
  
- **Maintain Up-to-Date Records**: Regularly update the asset details, especially if there are changes in location, assignee, or lifecycle stage, to maintain accurate records.

- **Utilize Attachments**: Use the attachments feature to store and access important documents related to the asset, facilitating quick retrieval and reference.

This structured approach to adding a new fixed asset ensures that all relevant information is captured, facilitating better asset management, tracking, and reporting within your organization.

## ‚úèÔ∏è Editing an Asset

To edit an existing asset:

1. Go to the **Fixed Asset** section.
2. Locate the asset you wish to edit.
3. Click on the three-dot menu next to the asset and select **"Edit."**
4. Update the asset's details as necessary, including name, code, depreciation settings, etc.
5. Click **"Save"** to apply the changes.

## üì† Generating Barcodes

The barcode feature allows the system to generate QR codes for individual assets or groups of assets, making it easier to access and manage asset data in real time. This is especially useful for on-site asset verification and management.

### üõ†Ô∏è How to Generate a Barcode

4. Print or download the QR code for use.
